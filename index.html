About

From mine sites in Ghana
to energy systems in Germany

I am a mechanical engineer from Tarkwa, Ghana, completing an MSc in Sustainable Energy Systems and Circular Process Engineering at Ruhr University Bochum. Before Bochum, I spent four years in industry — maintaining LPG infrastructure at Quantum Logistics and deploying Caterpillar telematics systems at Gold Fields Tarkwa Mine, one of West Africa's largest open-pit gold operations.

That background gives me something most energy modellers lack: I have worked in environments where equipment failure costs tens of thousands of dollars per hour — where data systems either work reliably or they do not. I carry that instinct for operational accountability into every model I build.

The move into sustainable energy was deliberate, and it connects naturally to the process engineering side of my MSc. Modern industries are fundamentally energy systems embedded within industrial processes — decarbonising them requires both. I apply the same rigour to all of it: real data, clear constraints, quantifiable outcomes. The projects here span renewable energy systems, power market modelling, and the technical foundations of circular industrial design. All work is version-controlled and openly published on GitHub.
